Three Weeks in Tokyo: What I Didn't Expect

📅 March 2025 ⏱ 8 min read

I arrived at Haneda at 6am on a Tuesday with a backpack, a pocket Wi-Fi, and absolutely no plan beyond the address of my guesthouse in Shimokitazawa. Three weeks later I left having walked somewhere between 200 and 300 kilometres and eaten things I still think about several times a week.

I'd been to Tokyo once before, briefly, for a conference. This was different. I had time. I had no schedule. And I had the nagging sense that Tokyo rewards people who are willing to wander without a destination.

A backstreet in Shimokitazawa, 7am, before the cafes opened.

The Transit System Is the Experience

Everyone tells you the Tokyo metro is efficient. What they don't tell you is that it's also — somehow — a place. I bought a Suica card on the first day and after that, getting anywhere felt effortless.

The Food, Obviously

The thing that struck me about eating in Tokyo wasn't the excellence of the top restaurants — it was the reliability of the ordinary ones.

🍜 If you find yourself in Shimokitazawa, the standing ramen bar two streets east of the south exit is open until midnight.

What I Didn't Expect

The thing I didn't expect was how quiet it is. Not silent — Tokyo is a city of 14 million people. But the social register is quiet.

Would I Go Back?

Already planning it. There's also unfinished ramen business.
