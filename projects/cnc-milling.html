Precision CNC Milling of Aluminium Fixture

📅 May 2025 🏫 University Capstone Project 🛠 Haas VF-2, SolidWorks, Mastercam

Overview

This project involved the design and manufacture of a precision aluminium fixture used to hold a sensor array during calibration. The primary challenge was achieving tight positional tolerances across multiple datum surfaces while working with the Haas VF-2 vertical mill available in the university machine shop.

💡 Key outcome: Final part held all critical tolerances within ±0.05 mm, verified by CMM inspection.

Figure 1 — Machine setup before first operation.

Design Process

The fixture was modelled in SolidWorks using a top-down design approach. All mounting holes were defined relative to a single primary datum to simplify the GD&T scheme and reduce cumulative error during machining.

Material Selection

6061-T6 aluminium was selected for its machinability, corrosion resistance, and sufficient stiffness. The table below summarises key material properties.

PropertyValueUnit
Yield Strength276MPa
Young's Modulus68.9GPa
Density2.70g/cm³
Thermal Conductivity167W/m·K

Manufacturing

Toolpaths were generated in Mastercam. A three-operation sequence was used: roughing, semi-finishing, and a final finishing pass at 0.1mm step-over to achieve Ra 1.6 μm on datum faces.

Inspection Results

CMM inspection on 8 critical features. All passed the ±0.05 mm requirement.

Figure 2 — CMM measured deviation from nominal (mm).

Conclusions

All 8 inspected features passed tolerance. Surface finish Ra 1.4 μm achieved against a target of Ra 1.6 μm. Total machining time: 3.5 hours.

  • All critical tolerances met (±0.05 mm)
  • Surface finish Ra 1.4 μm (target: Ra 1.6 μm)
  • Completed within material budget
